Apple Pie

Description

Apple pie is a sweet, baked dessert that consists of a wheat crust with a soft, syrupy apple-and-sugar filling. It is usually served warm and sometimes with a scoop of ice cream.

History

Although apple pie is considered an American icon, its origins actually lie in Europe. The first recorded recipe came from England during the late 1300s and also included figs, the spice saffron, raisins and pears. In addition to that, old versions of the dessert lacked sugar. Crusts were not eaten; they were simply meant to hold the filling. Furthermore, crab apples - perfect for making cider but unsuitable for pies - were the only types of apples in North America until European settlers planted apple trees, which later led to the breeding of new variations of apples.

Where to Taste

Apple pie can be found prepackaged at many North American grocery stores, such as Walmart and Freshco. These pies generally need only be baked as per the instructions on the box. You can also find recipes via baking websites and library cookbooks.
