This Week's Top Desserts #1

Daifuku

Alice Wiegand (Lyzzy). Daifuku1. April 4th, 2006. Commons.wikimedia.org. https://commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Daifuku_1.jpg#file

Description

Daifuku is a treat from Japan. It is chewy and powdery on the outside, with a sweet filling (usually red bean paste) on the inside. The size of daifuku can range from a diameter of 4 to 7 cm. This dessert is often served with green tea.

History

According to foodinjapan.org, a woman named Otayo invented daifuku and called it “Otama-mochi.” Later, it was changed to “daifukumochi.” Nowadays, there are many variations of this sweet treat, including daifuku with custard and pudding filling instead of red bean paste.

Where to Taste

Daifuku can be found in packages at Asian supermarkets. The Internet also has lots of recipes you can try!
